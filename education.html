Training & Education for Agrivoltaics

Why is education and training on agrivoltaics so important?

Building a sustainable future requires not only technological innovation but also a knowledgeable and engaged community. Our educational initiatives aim to empower the next generation of scientists, farmers, policymakers, and citizens with the skills and understanding to advance food-energy-water solutions.

School Garden Program

We partner with local schools to bring agrivoltaics concepts into the classroom and the schoolyard. These living laboratories provide hands-on learning experiences in science, technology, engineering, agriculture, and math (STEAM), connecting students directly with their food and energy sources.

Global School of Agrivoltaics

Through international workshops, online courses, and collaborative research projects, we are building a global network of agrivoltaics practitioners. This initiative facilitates knowledge exchange across different climates and cultures, accelerating the adoption of best practices worldwide.

Interdisciplinary Opportunities

At the university level, we provide research opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students from a wide range of disciplines, including geography, environmental science, engineering, and public policy. This interdisciplinary approach is crucial for solving the complex challenges at the food-energy-water nexus.
