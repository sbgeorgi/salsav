Flagship Research Sites & Initiatives

Highlighting key agrivoltaic research locations and collaborative projects led or significantly contributed to by the SALSA V team at the University of Arizona.

Biosphere 2 Agrivoltaics Lab

Arizona, USA

Pioneering dryland agrivoltaics research since 2017, focusing on food-energy-water nexus, crop yields, water conservation, and panel efficiency.

Learn More

AES Utility-Scale Agrivoltaics

Central Arizona, USA

Translating agrivoltaics to commercial solar farms with The AES Corporation. Testing grazing, conventional, and climate-adapted crops under utility-scale panels.

Learn More

Colorado Agrivoltaics (SCAPES)

Colorado, USA

Collaboration with CSU and NREL at sites like Jack’s Solar Garden, evaluating system designs for Colorado's semi-arid climate and diverse agriculture.

Learn More

Kenya Community Agrivoltaics

Makueni County, Kenya

Addressing food and energy poverty through community-led agrivoltaics, powering irrigation and enhancing crop yields in a semi-arid region.

Learn More

Israel Agrivoltaics (Arava Desert)

Negev, Israel

Developing agrivoltaic systems for extreme desert climates with the Kasser Joint Institute and Arava Institute, focusing on water savings and crop resilience.

Learn More

Morocco Agrivoltaics Collaboration

Morocco

Partnering with IRESEN and Green Energy Park to advance agrivoltaics research and pilot projects, adapting solutions for North African arid lands.

Learn More

Mexico Agrivoltaics Initiatives

Mexico

Collaborating on the PASE project with UNAM and exploring community-based agrivoltaics in Northern Mexico for arid land solutions and education.

Learn More

Key Contributors

Meet the dedicated researchers and collaborators driving the SALSA V initiatives at the University of Arizona and partner institutions.

Dr. Greg Barron-Gafford

Professor, School of Geography, Development & Environment; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona

Leads SALSA V. Research in biogeography, plant ecology, climate change impacts, and agrivoltaics. Pioneering FEW nexus solutions.

Dr. Andrea Gerlak

Professor, School of Geography, Development & Environment; Director, Udall Center for Studies in Public Policy, University of Arizona

Focuses on water governance, environmental policy, equity, institutional change, and adaptation. Co-PI on AES utility-scale project.

Jordan Macknick

Lead Energy-Water-Land Analyst, National Renewable Energy Laboratory

Expert in environmental impacts of energy tech, energy-ecology synergies, and agrivoltaics. Leads NREL's InSPIRE project.

Dr. Mitch Pavao-Zuckerman

Associate Professor, Environmental Science & Technology, University of Maryland

Research in urban ecology, green infrastructure, stormwater management, and ecosystem services. Collaborator on early Biosphere 2 agrivoltaics research.

Mary Werner

Research Program Administrator, School of Geography, Development, & Environment, University of Arizona

Manages research programs and administrative aspects within the Barron-Gafford lab and SGDE.

Dr. Holly Andrews

Postdoctoral Research Associate, School of Geography, Development, & Environment; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona

Ecosystem ecologist and biogeochemist. Research on sustainable agriculture, biosphere-atmosphere interactions, soil ecology, and the SCAPES Agrivoltaics Project.

Dr. Carrie Seay-Fleming

Postdoctoral Research Associate, Udall Center for Studies in Public Policy; School of Geography, Development, & Environment, University of Arizona

Environmental sociologist focusing on food system transformations, sustainability transitions, land-use conflict, and social aspects of solar development and agrivoltaics.

Tali Neesham-McTiernan

PhD Candidate, School of Geography, Development, & Environment; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona

Research merges physical and social sciences to map areas suitable for agrivoltaics in East Africa and the US. Interests: agrivoltaics, climate adaptation, GIS.

Tyler Swanson

Graduate Research Assistant, Udall Center for Studies in Public Policy; School of Geography, Development, & Environment, University of Arizona

M.A. student studying social aspects of agrivoltaics, agritourism, rural geography, sociotechnical transitions, and energy policy for sustainable rural development.

Alyssa Salazar

Research Associate, School of Geography, Development, & Environment; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona

Research Professional II. Leads research on agrivoltaic impacts on crop phenology and growing season patterns. Involved in desert farming experiments.

Caleb Ortega

Program Coordinator, School of Geography, Development, & Environment; Biosphere 2; School Garden Workshop, University of Arizona

Coordinates UA Community and School Garden Program. Supports agrivoltaics research at Biosphere 2 and local schools through site maintenance and planning.

Patrick Murphy

Research Specialist, School of Geography & Development; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona

Contributes to research efforts within the Barron-Gafford lab at Biosphere 2, focusing on agrivoltaics and related ecological studies.

Isaiah Barnett-Moreno

Undergraduate Research Assistant & Farm Manager, School of Geography, Development, & Environment; Biosphere 2, University of Arizona & Pima Community College

Manages research farm operations, involved in agrivoltaics research at Biosphere 2, and works with aquaponics for food accessibility.
